Setting the Timezone on Your Sensr.net Camera

  • October 28, 2011
  • Adam Beguelin
When you add a camera to Sensr.net we ask for your timezone, but sometimes folks forget to set it. It defaults to America/Los Angeles because that’s the home time zone of Sensr.net. If you want to change it, go to your camera page and click on the little gear in the upper right. Here’s a picture where I’ve circled the gear in red. This gear opens the settings panel. Simple Guide to Setting up a LOFTEK CXS 2200

  • October 17, 2011
  • Robert Whitefield

Last week I wrote a not-so-simple guide to setting up the LOFTEK CXS 2200, which really just turned into a long rant about my lack of common sense. So I figured it may be helpful to write a more straightforward guide with real steps rather than pictures of Gregory House, M.D.

Overview

  1.  Connect LOFTEK CXS 2200 to router via ethernet

  2. Determine your router’s IP address – important.

  3. Browse to the camera’s IP address (default: 192.168.0.178)

  4. Tell Sensr.net about your camera

  5. Tell your camera about Sensr.net via FTP settings

Though the LOFTEK CXS 2200 is a wireless network camera, you have to plug it in via ethernet in order to configure its wifi settings. Make sure the camera’s connected to a power source, too. Duh.
